01. Credit Basics

Understanding how credit works is the first step toward financial freedom. Your credit report is a history of your financial reliability.

The Five Pillars of Credit

  • Payment History (35%): Your record of on-time payments.
  • Amounts Owed (30%): Your credit utilization ratio.
  • Length of Credit History (15%): How long you've held accounts.
  • New Credit (10%): Recent inquiries and account openings.
  • Credit Mix (10%): The variety of credit types you use.

02. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

The FCRA is a federal law that regulates the collection of consumer credit information and access to your credit reports.

Your Core Rights

  • The Right to Accuracy: Consumer reporting agencies must ensure the information they report is accurate.
  • The Right to Dispute: You have the right to challenge inaccurate or unverifiable information.
  • The Right to Privacy: Only people with a "permissible purpose" can access your credit report.

03. Metro 2 Technical Standards

Metro 2 is the industry standard format for reporting consumer credit information. It ensures data is communicated consistently between different systems.

Why it Matters

Data furnishers must follow strict Metro 2 guidelines. When data is reported incorrectly—for example, if a "Date of Last Activity" doesn't align with other fields—it creates a compliance error under FCRA standards.
